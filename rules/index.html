Overview

Two players, each commanding a unique hero, battle using decks of themed cards. Deploy creatures, cast spells, attach equipment, and invoke powerful enchantments. Win by reducing your opponent's Life Points (LP) or Sanity Points (SP) to zero.

Components per player:

  • 1 Hero card
  • 15-card deck (chosen from hero's ~19-card pool before the match)
  • Access to a shared Marketplace (The Armory)

Setup

  1. Choose heroes. Each player selects a hero.
  2. Build decks. From your hero's card pool (~19 cards), choose 15 to form your deck. The remaining cards are set aside (not used this match).
  3. Draft the marketplace. Each player secretly picks 3 neutral card types they want available. Reveal picks simultaneously and merge into one list (overlapping picks just count once). Fill remaining slots with random draws until the marketplace has exactly 10 types, 2 copies each (20 cards total).
  4. Set starting resources. Each player sets LP and SP to their hero's starting values. Place the hero card face-up.
  5. Shuffle and draw. Shuffle your 15-card deck. Draw 5 cards.
  6. Determine first player. Flip a coin or choose randomly.

Win Conditions

You win when your opponent's LP reaches 0 (killed in combat) or their SP reaches 0 (driven to madness). Both are checked immediately whenever a resource changes. If both players would reach 0 simultaneously, the active player loses.

Card Types

Creatures

Permanent cards with ATK (attack power), DEF (health), and SV (sacrifice value). Creatures fight in combat and can be sacrificed for resources.

Summoning sickness: Creatures cannot attack the turn they enter play. They can block and use special abilities.

Spells

One-time effects. Two subtypes:

  • Cycled: After use, goes to your discard pile (will be reshuffled).
  • Burned: After use, removed from the game permanently. More powerful but irreplaceable.

Spells are normally played during your Main Phase. They may also be played during Reactive Windows on your opponent's turn — but any spell played reactively is permanently removed from the game (goes to the Removed zone), even if it would normally be Cycled. Exception: spells with the Reaction keyword follow their normal cycle/burn behavior when played reactively. See Reactive Play for full rules.

Equipment

Permanent attachments. Attach to a creature or your hero (as specified). One equipment per creature/hero. Attaching a new one sends the old one to discard. Equipment on a creature that leaves the field of play goes to your discard.

Enchantments

Permanent effects that stay on the battlefield. Some have upkeep costs.

Turn Structure

Each turn follows these phases in order:

1. Ready Step

  • Ready all your exhausted permanents (creatures, equipment).
  • Clear temporary buffs from last turn.
  • Apply passive hero powers and enchantment auras.
  • Process Bleed: Each creature with a bleed token takes 1 damage, then the token is removed. Your hero takes any stacked bleed damage, then it clears.

2. Draw Phase

  • Replenish: Draw cards until you have 5 in hand.
  • Mandatory draw: Draw 1 additional card (you always draw at least 1).
  • After this phase you have up to 6 cards in hand.

If your deck is empty when you need to draw, reshuffle your discard pile into a new deck and take Toll(2) cycle damage. If both deck and discard are empty, take 1 LP draw damage per missing card instead.

3. Income Step

  • Reset Essence to 0, then gain your hero's Essence per turn.
  • Add bonus Essence from enchantments, equipment, or other sources (e.g., Leyline Conduit, Bleed the Land).
  • Essence does NOT carry over between turns.

4. Upkeep Step

  • Pay upkeep costs for your permanents (Equipment, Enchantments).
  • If you can't or choose not to pay, that permanent is destroyed.

5. Main Phase

Take any number of actions in any order:

6. End Phase

  • Passive healing effects trigger (e.g., Iron Discipline).
  • Voluntary discard: You may discard any cards you don't want. Each discard: Toll(1).
  • Hand limit: If you still have more than 5 cards, discard down to 5. Each discard: Toll(1).
  • Feedback: Both players check for unspent non-income Essence. Each point from non-income sources (sacrifice, discard, exhaust abilities, hero powers): Toll(1) per point to that player.
  • Temporary buffs clear. All remaining Essence is lost.

Combat

Combat happens once per turn during your Main Phase.

1. Declare Attackers

Choose any number of your ready, non-summoning-sick creatures to attack. Each attacker becomes exhausted.

Attack triggers happen now (Horrify, Warhorse Knight buffs, etc.).

After attackers are declared, your opponent may use a Reactive Window before blockers are assigned.

2. Declare Blockers

Your opponent assigns their ready creatures as blockers. Each blocker blocks one attacker. Multiple blockers cannot block the same attacker. An attacker with no blocker deals damage to the defending hero.

Blocking restrictions:

  • Exhausted creatures cannot block.
  • Only creatures with Flying or Reach can block a Flying attacker.

3. Resolve Damage

All combat damage is simultaneous.

  • Blocked attackers: Attacker and blocker deal their ATK as damage to each other. Damage is applied to DEF (tracked as damage counters).
  • Unblocked attackers: Deal their ATK as LP damage to the defending hero.
  • Vampiric creatures that deal combat damage to a creature or hero heal that much LP to their controller.
  • Creatures reduced to 0 or fewer DEF die (go to owner's graveyard).

Reactive Play

Either player may play spells during two Reactive Windows on the opponent's turn:

  • Attack Window: After the active player declares attackers, before blockers are assigned.
  • Spell Window: After the active player casts a spell and pays its costs, before the spell resolves.

In physical play: after any such action, the non-active player says "I want to respond" before the action resolves. If no response is declared, the action proceeds.

Paying for Reactive Spells

Getting Essence on your opponent's turn requires planning ahead:

  • Exhausting creatures: Any ready creature you control with an Exhaust ability (e.g., "Exhaust: Gain 1 Essence") may be used during a Reactive Window. That creature is now exhausted — gain the benefit, but that creature cannot block.
  • Sacrificing creatures: You may sacrifice a creature during a Reactive Window and choose Offering (Essence) or Consume (SP) as normal.
  • SP-cost spells: Spells with no Essence cost (only SP) are naturally playable without any Essence income. Your SP pool is always accessible.
  • Temporal Cache / Spell Vault tokens: Pre-load tokens during your turns. Spend tokens as Essence during any Reactive Window.
  • Toll for Essence: You may Toll(1) to gain 1 Essence at any time — including during Reactive Windows. Universal fallback for funding reactive plays. Note: Toll-generated Essence is non-income Essence and subject to Feedback if unspent at end of turn.

Reactive Casting Rules

  • Any spell may be played during a Reactive Window if you can pay its cost.
  • One spell per window. The non-active player gets one chance to respond. That reactive spell resolves immediately — it does not open a new Reactive Window. There are no chains or stacks.
  • Spells played during a Reactive Window are permanently removed from the game (Removed zone), even if they would normally be Cycled. Exception: spells with the Reaction keyword follow their normal cycle/burn behavior when played reactively (a Cycled Reaction spell goes to discard, a Burned Reaction spell goes to Removed as usual).
  • Counter spells (e.g., Temporal Interrupt) cancel the triggering spell entirely — it does not resolve. The countered spell still goes to the appropriate zone (Removed if it was Burned, discard if Cycled). Both players lose their spell and any resources spent.
  • Creatures cannot be played directly from hand during Reactive Windows (they require Main Phase summoning). See Errata for card-specific exceptions.
  • Enchantments and Equipment cannot be played during Reactive Windows.

Sacrifice

During your Main Phase or a Reactive Window, you may sacrifice any creature you control. Choose one:

  • Consume: Recover SP equal to the creature's SV.
  • Offering: Gain Essence equal to the creature's SV.

Sacrifice triggers on the creature resolve first (e.g., Conscript's "deal 1 damage"). Then the creature goes to your graveyard.

The Marketplace (The Armory)

A shared pool of neutral cards available to both players.

Buying: During your Main Phase, pay a card's buy cost in Essence. The card goes to your hand. You can play it the same turn if you have the resources.

Stock: 2 copies of each card type. Once both copies are bought, that card is gone.

Draft (setup): Each player secretly picks 3 types. Merge into one list (overlapping picks just count once), then fill remaining slots with random draws to reach exactly 10 types. Always 10 types (20 cards) per game.

Special Rules

Cycle Damage

Every time you reshuffle your discard pile into a new deck: Toll(2). Burned spells and dead creatures thin your deck, accelerating reshuffles.

Damage on Creatures

Damage persists on creatures between turns. A creature with 3 DEF that takes 2 damage has 1 DEF remaining. It stays damaged until healed or it dies.

Discard Pain

Every discard during the End Phase: Toll(1) — whether voluntary or forced by the hand limit. Discarding cards due to card/creature effects during other phases is free.

Draw Damage

If you must draw but have no deck AND no discard pile, take 1 LP per missing card. This is not a Toll — it is LP damage only.

Equipment Swapping

One equipment per creature (or hero). Attaching a new equipment sends the old one to your discard pile.

Exhausting Equipment

When you exhaust an equipment card to activate its ability, only the equipment becomes exhausted. The creature or hero it is attached to is unaffected and remains ready.

LP and SP Maximum

LP and SP cannot exceed their hero's starting values. Any healing or recovery that would push a resource above its starting value is capped — the excess is lost. This applies to all sources: Vampiric combat damage, Consume (sacrifice), card effects, and any other gain.

Feedback

Essence gathered beyond your hero's income is volatile. At the end of every turn, both players check for unspent non-income Essence. Each point of unspent Essence from non-income sources (sacrifice, discard-for-Essence, Toll-for-Essence, exhaust abilities, hero powers): Toll(1) per point to that player. Essence from your Income Step (hero base + enchantment + equipment bonuses) is safe — you are not punished for unspent income. Then all remaining Essence is lost.

Spending order: Income Essence is always spent first. If you have 3 income Essence and 3 generated Essence but only spend 3 total, the 3 income is consumed — the 3 generated Essence remains unspent and triggers Feedback. You cannot choose to "spend" generated Essence first to avoid the penalty.

This applies to both players, not just the active player. If you sacrifice a creature during a Reactive Window on your opponent's turn and gain Essence via Offering, that Essence feeds back at the end of your opponent's turn if unspent. Every resource you actively generate must be spent or it hurts.

Unmade

Some card effects Unmake a creature instead of destroying it. An Unmade creature is placed in the Removed zone permanently — it does not go to the Graveyard. The creature has not died — no death triggers, sacrifice triggers, or any other leave-play effects fire. It cannot be returned to play by any effect (Grave Touch, Mass Reanimate, Blood Bond, or any other recursion). Only explicit card text that says "Unmake" causes this; normal destruction always sends creatures to the Graveyard.

Exception: A card whose trigger text explicitly names Unmake (e.g., Bone Tithe: "dies or is Unmade") fires when a creature is Unmade because the card itself says so — this is not a death trigger firing. Such cards are the only effects that acknowledge an Unmake.

Quick Reference

Turn order: Ready → Draw → Income → Upkeep → Main (combat inside) → End

Essence: Resets to 0 each turn. Gain from: hero income, exhausting creatures, discarding cards, sacrificing (Offering), hero powers, enchantments, Temporal Cache / Spell Vault / Bone Tithe tokens, Toll(1) (pay 1 LP or SP → gain 1 Essence, any time). Unspent non-income Essence: Toll(1) per point at end of every turn — checked for both players, not just the active player (Feedback).

SP costs: Spells, some enchantments, some hero powers. SP does not regenerate naturally. Recover via: Consume (sacrifice), specific card effects (Clarity, etc.).

Hand limit: 5 cards at end of turn. Draw to 5 + 1 extra at start. Every End Phase discard (voluntary or forced): Toll(1).

Reactive Windows: After opponent casts a spell (Spell Window) or declares attackers (Attack Window), you may play one spell in response. No chains — it resolves immediately. Counter spells cancel the triggering spell. Reactive spells are permanently removed from the game, unless they have the Reaction keyword (which lets them cycle/burn normally). Pay costs via SP pool, Temporal Cache / Spell Vault tokens, creature exhaustion, sacrifice, or Toll(1) for 1 Essence.

Unmade: Creatures removed by Unmake effects go to the Removed zone (not Graveyard). Cannot be recursed.

LP/SP maximum: Neither can exceed the hero's starting value. Excess healing or recovery is lost.

Win: Opponent LP = 0 or SP = 0.

Errata

Card-specific rulings that extend or clarify printed card text. Rules in this section apply only to the named card.

Spell Vault

Spell Vault allows cards stored under it to be played at any time on any player's turn — broader than the two standard Reactive Windows.

  • A creature deployed from Spell Vault on your opponent's turn enters play before your turn begins. Summoning sickness clears at the start of your turn, so the creature is ready to attack immediately when your turn starts.
  • Spells deployed from Spell Vault on your opponent's turn are permanently removed from the game (Removed zone) unless they have the Reaction keyword.
  • Creatures, Enchantments, and Equipment deployed from Spell Vault enter play immediately regardless of whose turn it is.
  • You may place a card under Spell Vault and fire it in the same action if stored tokens already cover the cost.
